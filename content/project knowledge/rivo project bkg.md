Overview

Rivo envisions a world where people can achieve daily financial goals leveraging the most convenient and secure blockchain technology.Born with the innovative DNA, it‚Äôs a platform to connect users‚Äô digital assets to the real world transactions, with the values of Customer first, Reliable and secure, Open& Innovative, Affordable.

Payment has been complicated, despite of recent years‚Äô innovations and integrations of big legacy systems. Rivo wants it to be simple, to be ‚ÄúApple-like‚Äù and like the stream of its brand, to be smooth.
Minimum education results from an experience that is finacially intuitive, which is called Fintuitive.

Working inside imToken app, Rivo is going to not only serve imToken but also serve many more other services like imToken. There are clear segregations while still some connection.

 

Design keywords

We abstract and break down all the important aspects from business goals and transform them into user-language keywords, which guide the end to end design.

 

Key guidelines

All the keywords above will pratically guide designers across Rivo iterations.

 

Motivation > Distraction
In each step of the payment process, though there are still inevitable distraction like switching from app to app, lack of trust at first, system pending, etc, we need to add certain motivation to keep user focused, and prevent them from leaving. From biz point of view, this is also important for conversion. This motivations could come from a proactive interaction that is similar to the real life financial behaviour, or a proactive interaction that provides sense of security.

 

End to end in context

Payment does not begin with payment and end with payment result. The moment user walks into a convenient store and notice the Rivo sticker on the counter has already began the payment process. We need to think comprehensively about each touchpoint in the ‚ÄúRivo service‚Äù and take them into experience consideration.

 

Clear and simple

Less is more, this is especially true during new product launch. There are only few seconds for new users to understand Rivo and its value. So each touchpoint design should be simplified.

 

Future ready

We design the service ready for potential future tech integration and device adaption, both to enrich and enhance experience. 
Two key elements from above directions:
The interactive dialog: the user interface port ready to connect with AI service and therefore connect user timely and informatively with ongoing tasks for smooth completion.
Multi form factor ready: Modular and content-oriented design, to be easily adapted with other devices than mobile phone.

Users interact with the UI*, UX, content, motion, service (all design elements) that deliver them the message that this service is backboned by web3 technology and now shaped in web2 format.

The stream branding of Rivo is vibrant, moving and continously transforming. collision Thinking and Explanation in design exploration
OVERALL DESIGN
Q:How to deliver a post-DPT experience like dev do underneath?

A: Experience design has several layers, of which there is the surface layer and underneath layer. Surface is what user can look, touch and feel, and that is something we deliver directly to users. At the same time, the structure of information beneath that is a layer that users dont feel in a obvious way.

Post DPT, we can do more things that pure fiat transaction and our value proposition to users shines more. That is more reflected on the surface layer experience which brings the direct impression. What we can do to make sure that we support post DPT functions is on underneath layer.

As whole, the 2 layers should convey the consistent brand characteristics through different stages.

This will largely impact the design of followings:

Look and feel, Content:  Rivo homepage, Onboarding

Process: Top up?, Payment

For other process like sign up and log in, they won;t be that different.

Supporting: Check all those DPT holders' apps' process, as well as the crypto apps. Those cultvate users' behaviour.

 

ONBOARDING & HOMEPAGE
Q:How to let users understand the relationship and connection between imToken and Rivo?

A: We go from stage by stage. Still, there has to be a CONNECTION for users to FEEL, which means that users dont go directly into a FIAT payment world without any EXPECTATION or MOTIVATION. 

Looking forward, the connection is still important cuz anyway we will march into the crypto-fiat payment stage, so users need to have that feeling at first that these stages are connected. It‚Äôs not like they see a XXpay in crypto world(not using GrabPay because that has a brand image), and then the XXPay becomes available for using crypto. It should be they see some fiat feature that can be linked to crypto world (UI, marketing, promotion, content).

 

 

Q:How to engage user to verify their imToken password when activating Rivo, without them concerning about security?

A: For these group of users. they are sensitive. So avoid using any design that shows the meaning of TRANSMISSION, EXCHANGE, etc. Instead, make them feel that the password is to protect themselves. 

From a sign up process point of view, adding that little bit of feeling of opportunity cost, may let me make decision fast.

 

 

Q:How to design a smooth and faster user experience in payment?

A: Basically, without all the substantial tech and structure wise change, design can only use different interaction status to MAKE USER FEEL AS IF ITS FAST. 

Getting down to executions, some TEMPORARY modules, will make user feel FASTER than whole page modules. Layers, pop-ups are some examples.

Another way is to distract user while waiting, adding ads, marketing, or educations can benefit.\

EXAMPLES can be made within iOS, like applepay vs safari in terms of page display.
